The Magic of Journaling

Do you have a journal? Do you write on it often? What do you write? Journaling has been existing around for years ago and does not only benefit its author, but it can also benefit generations. Like Anne Frank, The Diary of a Young Girl. The obstacles she lived had and have a historical impact on the world. Maybe, our dairies will not have that global influence but can influence our children, grandchildren, etc. Journaling has various benefits. Studies have shown that practicing this activity is like therapy. Journaling helps organize and clear our thoughts; it improves physical and physiological health, especially after stressful events. Also, it develops communication skills like writing and speaking; and working memory. If journaling is done before going to sleep, it decreases cognitive stimulus, facilitating falling asleep. These are some of the benefits, but as the phycologist, John Dewey said, ‘We do not learn from experience…we learn from reflecting on experience.’ While writing feelings, we also reflect on what we are thing and processing in our mind. It allows us to reflect on what we have lived and learned. We can write about good and bad experiences the outcome will be the same we will feel better.
