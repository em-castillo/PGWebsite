The benefists of Exercise

A way to reduce stress is exercising. This does not mean you need to run a marathon or spend several hours in the gym. The exercise involves moving your body. You can dance, go for a walk, run, workout, ride your bike or play tennis. Do what you like most! The goal is to enjoy it. When we are under stress, our muscles tense, especially in the neck, shoulders, upper back, and even can cause headaches. This discomfort can lead to more stress, affecting our mind and body. Exercising helps to relax the muscles and relieve tension in the body. How our mind and body are connected, if one feels better, the other one will too. Getting some exercise is also a good habit to include in your routine. It can decrease depression, increase your mood, and academic performance. It is a free and easy way to make your life less stressed, happier, and healthier.
